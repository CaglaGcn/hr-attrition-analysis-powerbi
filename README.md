# Employee Attrition Analysis | Power BI

## Project Objective

This project analyzes employee attrition patterns to identify which employee groups are more likely to leave the company and which risk factors are associated with higher attrition.

The dashboard follows a general-to-specific approach: starting with workforce overview, then attrition analysis, risk segmentation, and finally actionable retention recommendations.

## Tools Used

- Power BI
- DAX
- Power Query
- Data Visualization
- HR Analytics

## Key Questions

- Which employee groups are more likely to leave?
- Which departments and job roles have higher attrition?
- Which risk factors are associated with employee attrition?
- Can we create an explainable risk score for retention planning?
- Which active employees should be prioritized for retention actions?

## Dashboard Pages

1. **Cover**  
   Project introduction and analysis flow.

2. **Workforce Overview**  
   General company structure including department, gender, age group, marital status, business travel and job level.

3. **Attrition Analysis**  
   Attrition breakdown by gender, marital status, overtime, age group, department/job role, income level and years at company.

4. **Risk Analysis**  
   Explainable risk score based on selected attrition indicators.

5. **Active High-Risk Employees**  
   List of active employees classified as High Risk for retention action planning.

6. **Conclusion & Recommended Actions**  
   Business recommendations for reducing employee attrition.

## Risk Score Methodology

Each employee received 1 point for each risk factor:

- Overtime = Yes
- Job Satisfaction <= 2
- Age < 30
- Business Travel = Travel Frequently
- Marital Status = Single

Risk levels:

- 0–1 points = Low Risk
- 2–3 points = Medium Risk
- 4–5 points = High Risk

## Key Insights

- Overall attrition rate is around 16%.
- Attrition is not evenly distributed across the company.
- Younger employees, frequent travelers, employees working overtime and employees with lower job satisfaction show higher attrition risk.
- High-risk employees have a significantly higher attrition rate than low-risk employees.
- Active high-risk employees can be used as a retention action list for HR teams.

## Recommended Actions

- Monitor overtime levels monthly.
- Rebalance workload for employees with frequent overtime.
- Review role fit and team-related issues for employees with low job satisfaction.
- Create mentoring and career development plans for early-career employees.
- Reduce travel burden through rotation or flexible work support.
- Prioritize retention actions in R&D and Sales teams.

## Business Value

This dashboard helps HR move from reactive attrition reporting to proactive retention planning by identifying and supporting active high-risk employees before they leave.
